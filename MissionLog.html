When we started Aerospace Club, we were given an assignment to design a supply pod that transport supplies to the astronauts. So for the first few weeks, we created several designs of the pod.
After we finished drawing our design, we started to created the design on computer. The computer design give us a clear picture of how we wanted our final design to look like. We design the supply pod, itself, as a sphere. Testing the sphere to ensure that it could roll on the moon's surface and can protect the astronauts' foods.
Inside our supply pod will be a box. The box inside will carry the astronaut's food. So the pod will protect the box when it's travelling from Earth to Moon. The box, itself, will have the food inside.
From January to February, we started creating the computer simulation. The computer simulation help give us a clear picture on how our pod will reacted when it landed on the moon surface and when it enters in the cracters.
On February 14, 2020, we started the bowling experiment. The bowling experiment help us determine how our pod will reacted to the moon surface. When the ball has no styrofoam around it to reduce the friction. The bowling ball didn't roll over the rocks that on the mat and it kept rolling on its side. However, when we added the styrofoam onto te bowling ball. The bowling ball was able roll over the rocks and the styrofoam was able to slow down enough to land properly.

Stage 6: The Final Prototype
This is our final design. In order to see if the added outer layer will provide more friction and reduce velocity. We took a bowling ball and calculated the speed on an incline with sand. We then altered the bowling ball and repeated the same test to find the new velocity.We found that the added material to the bowling ball did increase the friction and reduced velocity.
